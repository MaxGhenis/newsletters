Major milestone

PolicyEngine technology now powers analysis at the UK Prime Minister's office

The same open-source microsimulation that powers PolicyEngine US is now being used at 10 Downing Street.

"Decision makers will have a new library of information they can turn to when the policy process moves too fast."

Nikhil Woodruff

Co-founder & CTO, PolicyEngine

Our co-founder and CTO Nikhil Woodruff has spent the past six months working as an Innovation Fellow with 10DS—the data science team at 10 Downing Street—adapting PolicyEngine's open-source microsimulation for government use.

The 10DS team built a package called 10ds-microsim on top of PolicyEngine that can rapidly estimate the impacts of policy reforms on living standards and distributional outcomes—providing decision-makers with faster access to analysis when the policy process moves too quickly for traditional approaches.

This demonstrates what open-source policy modelling can achieve at the highest levels of government—and it's the same core technology that powers PolicyEngine US.

What this means for you

The same transparent, reproducible microsimulation technology that governments are choosing to adopt is what powers all your PolicyEngine US analyses—from household calculators to nationwide policy impacts.

Why government analysts chose PolicyEngine

Modern Python framework

Unlike legacy tools built on proprietary or niche technologies, PolicyEngine uses Python—the language of choice for data scientists across government and academia.

ML-powered accuracy

Machine learning-based imputation and calibration techniques deliver accuracy that exceeds what conventional microsimulation methods can achieve.

Transparent and reproducible

Freely available code meant the 10DS team could adapt it, validate it against internal benchmarks, and build infrastructure—without proprietary licensing constraints.

Open source at the heart of government

We believe government analysis should be built on transparent, reproducible foundations. Seeing PolicyEngine's technology contribute to decision-making at the centre of UK government is a validation of that vision.
