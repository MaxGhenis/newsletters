Today, PolicyEngine is thrilled to announce the beta launch of state income tax modeling for all 50 states and Washington D.C. This significant milestone, made possible by the hard work and dedication of our team of over 100 open-source contributors, expands our existing suite of policy analysis tools, which already includes a comprehensive model of federal income taxes and benefit programs like SNAP, SSI, and WIC.


What This Means for You üîç


With this launch, anyone can now use PolicyEngine's free, open-source platform to:

  • Enter their household information and estimate their state income taxes, along with federal taxes and benefit amounts.

  • Access free analytics, such as how net income and marginal tax rates change with respect to earnings.

  • Design customizable policy reforms for state income taxes and estimate the impact on households and society, including changes to the budget, poverty rates by age, race, and gender, income inequality, the distribution of benefits and costs, and impacts by income decile.

This launch is just the beginning. Later this year, we'll be rolling out even more enhancements, including:

  • Full validation against NBER's TAXSIM model for all states and tax years

  • More recent tax law, including legislation that defines tax rules for 2024 and beyond

  • The ability to model how federal reforms affect state taxes


We're also actively seeking support to extend our recently beta-launched microdata enhancement to the state level, further improving the accuracy of state impact estimates for both state and federal reforms.
